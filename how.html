Design for Everyone. Build for Everyone

1. Semantic HTML

Using modern semantic html tags makes it easier for screen readers and assistive tech to understand the content of a website. When semantic structure is used correctly, websites become easier to navigate, more consistent, and more compatible across devices and assistive tools.

2. Incorporate accessibility

Build accessibility into the process instead of it being an afterthought.

This can be done by understanding your users can considering a whide range of abilities. Another way to do this is to test with real people and invovle individual with diverse abilities to ensure multiple groups are being considered.

3. Color & Contrast

People with color blindness or partial blindness have a hard time reading content with low contrast on a screen. Provide high contrast themes.
